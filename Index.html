Welcome & The OJI Transition
What changed after your first week
⌄
The Core ConceptHearing instructions about a route and understanding a route after carrying it are two completely different things. This manual is written for the version of you that now exists after that first week.

What the Learning Curve Actually Looks Like

  • Weeks 1–2: Survival mode — everything feels hard. Focus entirely on safety, accuracy, and showing up. Speed is not the goal yet.
  • Weeks 3–5: Pattern recognition — routes start making sense. You develop personal systems for organization.
  • Weeks 6–10: Confidence builds — casing gets faster, parcel sequencing improves. Recovery from setbacks gets easier.
  • Weeks 11–13: Consistency — your professional record is being built. Attendance, communication, and accuracy are being watched. This window matters.
What Experienced Carriers RespectNot speed. They respect honesty about what you do and don't know, good-faith effort, asking questions before guessing, and communicating problems early instead of hiding them.
RememberEvery experienced carrier went through exactly what you are going through. Route memory takes weeks, months, and eventually years. The goal in week one is accuracy and safety — not speed.

Your Role as a CCA
What "City Carrier Assistant" actually means
⌄
Most Important Mindset ShiftIf your schedule changed, you got an unfamiliar route, or you're working Sunday — you are NOT failing. That IS the CCA position. Understanding this is one of the most important things in this manual.
  • Schedule changes are normal — your assignment may change week to week. This is expected.
  • Different routes are normal — you will carry routes you are not familiar with. That is part of the job.
  • Sundays and holidays are normal — CCAs work package delivery on Sundays and holidays regularly.
  • Short notice is normal — call-offs and coverage assignments happen frequently.
  • Unfamiliar neighborhoods are normal — use your map, scanner, and ask questions.
Reframe It"I keep getting different routes" does not mean "I'm failing." It means the system is working as designed. A difficult route is an opportunity to show your value.
What You Are BuildingMulti-route familiarity makes you extremely valuable. Every consistent day as a CCA builds seniority toward career conversion — a bid route, set schedule, full benefits, and protections CCAs don't yet have. That is the goal.

Rockford Geography
Address logic, quadrant system, ZIP codes
⌄
The Two DividersRock River = East/West divider. State Street (Business US-20) = North/South divider. Addresses begin at the 100 block near each divider and increase outward. The 800 block is farther from the divider than the 400 block.
NW = W addresses, N of State St
NE = E addresses, N of State St
SW = W addresses, S of State St

Quadrant Notes

NW — 61101 & 61103Older neighborhoods, mounted delivery sections, irregular street patterns. Know your arrow key access points.
NE — 61107 · 61111 · 61114 · 61115Highest daily parcel volumes in Rockford. Complex apartment layouts and subdivisions. Verify names on apartment mail carefully.
SW — 61102Dense park-and-loop sections, older residential grid. Winter walking hazards are common — slow your pace on ice.
SE — 61104 · 61108 · 61109Industrial and mixed routes. Older apartments with higher turnover — careful name verification required. Business routes often have specific delivery windows.
Quick Test: If you are on N Main St delivering to 800 N Main and the next address is 1100 N Main — you are moving away from State Street, northward. The numbers tell you the direction.

Vehicle-Specific Notes
  • Promaster / Ford Transit: Floor in delivery sequence, small parcels on shelving.
  • NGDV: Use the cubby system per the MDD section number called.

Before Leaving the Office

  • Case all mail — check hot case for first-class mis-sorts
  • Verify all accountables are in your possession
  • Load vehicle in delivery sequence
  • Check vehicle condition — report any issues to management before leaving
  • Check Package Lookahead on MDD for any large parcels

Common Mistakes

Scanning too earlyScan at delivery point only — never in the vehicle
Waiting too long to callCall by 1:30 PM if behind — not at 5 PM
Random parcel placementAlways load in delivery sequence
Not securing vehicleBrake, curb wheels, engine off, lock — every stop
Hiding mailFederal offense — report to supervisor, never hide

Route Integrity — When You Return

  • Remove all loose mail from the vehicle — check under seats and cargo floor
  • Bundle and endorse throwbacks accurately before returning
  • Keep UBBM completely separate from First-Class returns
  • Leave a note about route-specific issues: aggressive dogs, blocked CBUs, vacant buildings, forwarding issues
RememberWhen you carry another carrier's route, your work directly impacts their next day. Leave it the way you would want to receive it.

How to Endorse Mail

Always Include: Your initials + route number + reason code (e.g., LS R12 VAC)
Individual Method
NMR, VAC, REF — ONLY 1 OR 2 PIECES		Write on EVERY piece
Bundle Method
ANK, IA, NSN — multiple pieces		Top piece only. Rubber-band the rest behind it.
Forwarding — When Unsure: Ask or HoldNever guess on forwarding. A wrongly forwarded piece can be lost for weeks. A misdelivered piece creates a confidentiality issue & a customer complaint. When in doubt — ask your supervisor.

MDD & Emergency Protocols
Tech, safety hazards, when things go wrong
⌄
Golden Rule of EmergenciesThe earlier you communicate, the more protected you are. Honest mistakes are almost always fixable. Hidden mistakes become disciplinary disasters.

MDD Scanner — Key Functions

  • Package Lookahead: After each relay loop, check this screen to verify no parcels were missed.
  • Start / End of Delivery: Must be scanned accurately. Do not falsify times.
  • Parcel Scan: Scan at the delivery point — never in the vehicle.
  • Accountable Items: Must be scanned at time of delivery or attempt. Never skip.
  • Dog Alert: Recording a dog issue helps future carriers and supports safety documentation.

✅ If You Are Running Behind

  • Stay calm and deliver safely
  • Notify management by 1:30 PM
  • Keep mail organized
  • Ask for assistance early

❌ Never Do This

  • Hide or dump mail
  • Falsify scan times or locations
  • Skip safety procedures
  • Wait until after dark to call

✅ Dog Incident

  • Stay calm, leave the area
  • Keep dog spray AND dog horn on you at all times
  • Use your satchel as a shield
  • Report all incidents same day

❌ Dog Incident — Never

  • Escalate or argue with customer
  • Turn your back on and run from a dog
  • Skip the incident report

Vehicle Breakdown or Accident

  • Secure the vehicle immediately — brake, neutral, wheels curbed
  • Call the station and your supervisor immediately
  • Call 911 first if anyone is injured
  • Do not move the vehicle until instructed to do so
  • Document everything with photos if safe
  • Complete PS Form 1769 (Accident Report) before end of tour
Never Falsify ScansScanner pressure from management never justifies falsification. Scanning in the vehicle instead of at the delivery point is a federal offense and grounds for termination.

What Good Communication Looks Like
When to call, what to say, how specific to be
⌄
Most Common MistakeNotifying management at 5:30 PM that you needed help at 2:00 PM is not communication — it is damage control. Early communication gives everyone options. Late communication eliminates them.

When to Call

SituationWhen to Call
Estimating 1+ hour overBy 1:00–1:30 PM — not at 5 PM
Unfamiliar apartment layoutWhen you first realize — not after wasting time
Dog confrontation or biteImmediately after the incident
Vehicle problemAs soon as you notice it
Misdelivered a pieceSame day, before leaving
Feeling physically unwellImmediately — do not push through
Customer becomes threateningLeave the area and call immediately
May need help finishing routeEarly afternoon — never after dark

Phrases You Can Use Right Now

"I'm estimating about one hour over. I'm on [street] right now. I have [X] stops, relays, or streets left."
"I have an apartment complex at [address] I'm not familiar with. Can you confirm the access code or get me the 3849 card?"
"I have a dog issue at [address]. Dog came through the gate. No injury, but I need the address flagged."
"I may need assistance. I'm at [location] with approximately [X] parcels remaining. My estimated finish is [time]."
"I have a vehicle issue — [describe it]. I'm parked safely at [location]."
"I believe I misdelivered a piece to [address]. The correct address is [address]. I can attempt recovery on my way back."
"I'm not feeling well and may need to come in. I'm at [location] right now."
Why Specificity Matters"I might be late" gives management nothing to work with. "I'm estimating 1 hour over, currently at 1400 N Main, have 45 parcels remaining" gives them everything they need to decide whether to send help. Specific communication protects you and improves outcomes.

Your First 90 Days
Probation, attendance, physical survival
⌄
Attendance Is the #1 Termination ReasonDuring probation, the contract provides less protection for CCAs. Missing days — even for legitimate reasons — can create a pattern management uses to justify termination. Call in early if you must be absent — well before your report time.
Emergency Annual Leave — Documentation RequiredIf you call off using Emergency Annual Leave, medical documentation is required. FMLA protections are limited during probation.

Physical Survival Tips

Invest in quality waterproof boots
Carry extra socks
Hydrate before work — not just during
Sleep is non-negotiable
Pace yourself — overexertion causes injury
Body needs 1–2 weeks to adapt to weather extremes
Avoiding BurnoutThe goal is not to impress anyone in week one. The goal is to still be there in week thirteen — accurate, safe, consistent, and improving. Pace yourself. The marathon matters more than the sprint.
Asking for Help Is a SkillThe carriers who develop fastest are not the ones who figure everything out alone. They identify problems quickly, ask the right questions, and implement feedback. Use every experienced carrier near you as a resource.

Rights & Contract Basics
Weingarten, breaks, overtime, documentation
⌄
Weingarten Rights — Say This Exactly "If this discussion could in any way lead to discipline, I respectfully request union representation before proceeding."
Breaks You Are Entitled ToTwo paid 10-minute rest breaks + one unpaid 30-minute lunch + reasonable comfort stops for restroom and hydration. These are contractual rights, not management favors. You do not have to skip meals or bathroom stops to meet informal time expectations.
Over 8 hrs/day or 40/week = 1.5x pay
Over 12 hrs/day or 60/week = 2.5x pay
Schedule posted every Wednesday

When to Contact the Union

  • Any meeting that could lead to discipline
  • Any formal or informal discussion about your performance
  • If you receive any written notice of discipline
  • Any on-the-job injury
  • If you believe you are being harassed or targeted
  • Any time you are unsure of your rights
Document EverythingScreenshot your schedule every Wednesday — check it daily after it's posted. Track your hours every pay period. Save all discipline notices and give copies to your union rep immediately. Report injuries on PS Form 1769 the same day they occur.
Never Sign Without UnderstandingDo not sign any document you do not understand — ask questions. Ask for a copy and consult your steward immediately after. You cannot be disciplined for requesting union representation — that right is federally protected.

Safety Rights

  • You have the right to refuse work that creates imminent danger to your safety
  • Report all dog incidents, slip/fall hazards, and safety concerns immediately
  • Document injuries the same day — delays in documentation weaken workers' comp claims
  • Heat illness and extreme cold injuries are legitimate safety issues, not excuses

Benefits, MBA & Pay
Health insurance, LiteBlue, MBA, career conversion
⌄
CCA Health Insurance — 60-Day WindowYou have 60 days from hire to enroll. Use PS Form 3117 — January 2025 version (or most current). Old forms are rejected. Mail via Certified Mail to HRSSC in Greensboro, NC. Never rely solely on a fax. See the CCA Insurance Packet for full instructions.
LiteBlue: liteblue.usps.gov
  • ePayroll — review every pay stub every pay period
  • Virtual Timecard — verify your clock rings match actual hours
  • PostalEASE — manage direct deposit and benefits elections
  • eOPF — your electronic personnel folder
⚠ Never share your EIN and password. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication immediately.
Work Injuries & OWCP — EComp.govAll workplace injury claims and OWCP filings go through EComp.gov. Document the injury on PS Form 1769 the same day it occurs.
NALC MBA — nalcmba.orgAn independent organization offering supplemental financial products designed specifically for letter carriers — separate from your USPS benefits. Includes short-term disability (critical for CCAs with limited leave), term life insurance, 20-year term plans, and retirement-focused plans built around FERS pension timing.
Scan to enroll or learn more · nalcmba.org
EAP — Free & Confidential — 1-800-327-4968Free support for you and your family: stress, anxiety, depression, financial pressure, grief, trauma, substance concerns. Using the EAP does not affect your employment. Available any time.

After Career Conversion — What Unlocks

  • PSHB: Full Postal Service Health Benefits program enrollment
  • TSP: Federal 401(k) with government matching — start as early as possible
  • FERS Pension: Defined benefit retirement based on years of service and salary
  • FEGLI: Federal Employees Group Life Insurance
  • Annual Leave: Up to 26 days/year · Sick Leave: up to 104 hrs/year
  • Bid route + set schedule — 11 paid holidays
Key PointEvery consistent day as a CCA moves you closer to that conversion date. Time is your biggest asset when it comes to TSP and pension — start contributions as early as possible after conversion.
